Machine Learning

In this module, I shall...

  • Learn about the key paradigms and algorithms in machine learning.
  • Get an understanding of data analytics based on machine learning and using modern programming tools, such as Python or R.
  • Experience how machine learning and data analytics can be used in real-world applications
  • Acquire the ability to gather and synthesise information from multiple sources to aid in the systematic analysis of complex problems using machine learning tools and algorithms.

On completion of this module, I will be able to...

  • Articulate the legal, social, ethical, and professional issues faced by machine learning professionals.
  • Understand the applicability and challenges associated with different datasets for the use of machine learning algorithms.
  • Apply and critically appraise machine learning techniques to real-world problems, particularly where technical risk and uncertainty is involved.
  • Systematically develop and implement the skills required to be effective member of a development team in a virtual professional environment, adopting real-life perspectives on team roles and organisation.

Reflections on Project Report

When it comes to the learning outcomes, I believe I fully achieved the first. Our team struggled a bit with figuring out what environment would be best to code with, but Google Colab ended up being our solution. After that, the coding itself was smooth sailing, with little to no difficulties. However, the second learning outcome is what I need to reflect upon most.

When creating this report, I noticed that our team struggled to stay on schedule. We only came together approximately 2 weeks before the deadline of the assignment, and that was because one of our group members took the initiative to be the leader and organize some ideas. I quickly worked with this individual to come up with a few business questions, however after this initial work was done, it took a long time before any other movements were made. A few of us had some real life obligations that could not be avoided. So, as a group we failed to correctly organize our time and maintain a good schedule to keep everyone included and able to contribute. We rushed our work a bit towards the end, which I feel can be felt in the end quality of the report. Next time I work in a team, I will do better to try and establish a schedule that works for everyone, despite the fact that I am uncomfortable in a leadership role and struggle to take initiative. This is something that I need to work on, as I realized while working on this assignment.

Jaccard Coefficient Calculations

Objective: Calculate the Jaccard Coefficient Calculations in a given table.

Learning Outcomes

  • Articulate the legal, social, ethical and professional issues faced by machine learning professionals.
  • Understand the applicability and challenges associated with different datasets for the use of machine learning algorithms.

I was given the following table and told to calculate the Jaccard Coefficient for the following pairs: "Jack and Mary", "Jack and Jim", and "Mary and Jim". To properly calculate the Jaccard coefficient, first I discarded the gender column and transformed the rest of the table into binaries.

After converting the table into binaries, we calculate the intersection between each pair. For example, Jack and Mary both have Fever and Test, so they have an intersection of 2. Jack and Jim have an intersection of 2 as well, and so does Mary and Jim. We also need to find out the union between the pairs. According to the binary table, Jack and Mary have 5 total attributes. Jack and Jim have 4, and Mary and Jim have 6. The Jaccard coefficients are as follows: Jack and Mary = 2/5 = 0.4, Jack and Jim = 2/4 = 0.5, Mary and Jim = 2/6 ≈ 0.333.
